Define Reflection

The definition given here is the most common one, but there are other interesting approaches.

For instance, the PCMI online course begins from assumptions that are more basic:

  1. For any rigid motion, the image of a line, ray or segment is, respectively, a line, ray or segment.
  2. For any line `m`, there is a rigid motion `R_m` called reflection across `m`. This `R_m` fixes every point of `m` but no other points are fixed.

From these two assumptions the PCMI approach is to prove what we're taking as the definition of reflection: that for every point `P` not on `m`, `m` is the perpendicular bisector of segment `overline(PR_m(P))`.
